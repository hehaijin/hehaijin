3D Conway's Game of Life

The Game of Life, also known simply as Life, is a cellular automaton devised by the British mathematician John Horton Conway in 1970. From Wikipedia

In more detail, the game is a grid of cells, each having a condition of alive or dead. In the common 2D version, each cell interacts with 8 neighbors. At each step, the 8 neighbors will determine whether the middle cell survives or not, based on simple rules like how many of the 8 neighbors are alive in this step.

Here it's 3D version, where each cell interactions with 26 cells.

And JavaFx is used to show the cell dynamics.
